## Churchill — repartees and reductios (training memory, well-attested)

1. Lady Astor: "If I were married to you, I'd put poison in your coffee." Churchill: "Nancy, if I were married to you, I'd drink it." — *(literal counter — gold standard for technique 9)*
2. Bessie Braddock: "Winston, you are drunk, and disgustingly drunk." Churchill: "Bessie, my dear, you are ugly, and disgustingly ugly. But tomorrow I shall be sober and you will still be ugly." — *(counter + escalation)*
3. "An empty taxi drew up to Downing Street, and Clement Attlee got out." — *(reductio ad rem absurdam — gold standard for technique 10)*
4. "Attlee is a modest man with much to be modest about." — *(antithetical + faint praise)*
5. "Attlee is a sheep in sheep's clothing." — *(antithetical of the wolf-in-sheep idiom)*

## Dorothy Parker — reductios and pickup-and-escalate

1. Clare Booth Luce: "Age before beauty." Parker: "Pearls before swine." — *(pickup-and-escalate — gold standard for technique 12, takes "before" and reapplies to "swine")*
2. "She runs the gamut of emotions from A to B." — On Katharine Hepburn — *(reductio)*
3. "The woman speaks eighteen languages and can't say no in any of them." — *(reductio + eccezione)*
4. "If all the girls at the Yale prom were laid end to end, I wouldn't be at all surprised."
